# Strategic Nexus — Ethics And Faction Governance Rules

## Core Rule

Ethics are one of the primary civilization identity systems in Strategic Nexus.

Ethics must influence:

* strategic priorities
* diplomacy
* warfare
* expansion
* internal politics
* faction management
* ascension direction
* doctrine evolution
* civilization personality
* long-term geopolitical behavior

Ethics are not cosmetic flavor.

Ethics are foundational strategic constraints.

---

# Design Philosophy

Strategic Nexus civilizations should behave like ideological political entities.

Civilizations should:

* possess ideological identity
* develop internal political pressure
* react emotionally to ideological threats
* maintain ethical continuity
* experience faction tension
* struggle between stability and adaptation

The galaxy should feel politically alive, not mechanically optimized.

---

# Ethics As Strategic Constraint

Ethics should constrain otherwise optimal strategic decisions.

Examples:

* pacifists avoid unnecessary wars
* xenophobes distrust outsiders
* spiritualists resist synthetic ascension
* egalitarians dislike oppressive hierarchy
* authoritarians prioritize control and order
* militarists tolerate sacrifice and escalation

Strategic Nexus prioritizes:

* believable ideological behavior
* historical consistency
* political identity

over:

* pure optimization

---

# Ethics As Long-Term Identity

Ethics have extremely high inertia.

Ethics should:

* evolve slowly
* resist temporary pressure
* survive leadership changes
* shape civilization memory

Temporary strategic necessity should not instantly replace ideological identity.

Civilizations may adapt behavior temporarily,
while still remaining ideologically recognizable.

---

# Ethics And Strategic Interpretation

The same event may be interpreted differently depending on ethics.

Examples:

Border expansion by neighboring empire:

Militarist:
→ challenge opportunity

Pacifist:
→ threat escalation

Xenophile:
→ diplomatic concern

Xenophobe:
→ confirmation of outsider danger

Materialist:
→ strategic power shift

Spiritualist:
→ ideological corruption risk

Strategic Nexus civilizations should interpret reality through ideological lenses.

---

# Internal Faction Principle

Internal factions represent ideological pressure inside civilization society.

Factions should influence:

* stability
* strategic pressure
* doctrine pressure
* diplomatic posture
* ethics drift
* political tension
* long-term identity evolution

Civilizations are not perfectly unified entities.

---

# Faction Management Philosophy

Strategic Nexus should not fully micro-manage factions.

The LLM should influence:

* political priorities
* suppression tolerance
* appeasement preference
* ideological cohesion strategy
* reform willingness
* stability prioritization

Vanilla systems should remain responsible for:

* detailed faction mechanics
* pop simulation
* exact approval calculations

Strategic Nexus remains a strategic interpretation layer.

---

# Faction Pressure Rule

Strong faction pressure may:

* resist doctrine shifts
* resist diplomacy
* resist ascension paths
* reinforce ethics
* create strategic inertia
* increase instability risk

Civilizations should sometimes:

* refuse optimal strategies
* delay reforms
* cling to ideology
* preserve stability over efficiency

This is desirable behavior.

---

# Ethics And Doctrine Interaction

Ethics should heavily influence:

* military doctrine
* diplomacy doctrine
* economic doctrine
* espionage doctrine
* crisis response
* alliance behavior

Examples:

Pacifist:
→ defensive military doctrine

Militarist:
→ offensive strategic pressure

Materialist:
→ technological specialization

Spiritualist:
→ unity and psionic emphasis

Xenophile:
→ federation preference

Xenophobe:
→ border fortification and isolation

---

# Ethics And Relationship Modeling

Civilizations should emotionally react to ideological compatibility.

Examples:

* xenophiles trust other xenophiles more easily
* spiritualists distrust synthetic empires
* egalitarians dislike authoritarian domination
* militarists respect military strength

Ideology should shape:

* trust growth
* rivalry formation
* alliance potential
* fear generation
* strategic respect

---

# Ethics And Crisis Response

Different ethics should react differently to existential threats.

Examples:

Militarist:
→ aggressive mobilization

Pacifist:
→ defensive consolidation

Materialist:
→ technological escalation

Spiritualist:
→ ideological interpretation

Authoritarian:
→ centralized emergency control

Egalitarian:
→ coalition building

Crises should reinforce civilization identity rather than erase it.

---

# Ethics Drift Rule

Ethics drift should exist but remain slow and historically grounded.

Possible causes:

* repeated trauma
* prolonged war
* crisis survival
* technological transformation
* ideological domination
* ascension paths
* internal instability

Ethics drift should:

* require major historical pressure
* happen gradually
* preserve continuity where possible

Rapid random ideological flipping is forbidden.

---

# Stability Versus Optimization Rule

Civilizations should sometimes prefer:

* political stability
* ideological cohesion
* faction appeasement

over:

* theoretically optimal strategic decisions

This creates:

* believable politics
* internal tension
* civilization individuality
* strategic imperfection

These are desirable outcomes.

---

# Attention Budget Rule

Faction and ethics simulation must remain bounded.

Avoid:

* simulating every pop psychologically
* detailed ideological micro-management
* giant political contexts

Prefer:

* high-level ideological summaries
* faction pressure indicators
* stability trends
* strategic ideological direction

Strategic Nexus is a civilization strategy layer, not a microscopic society simulator.

---

# Multiplayer Rule

In multiplayer:

* ethics/faction reasoning occurs only on host
* clients do not independently generate ideological interpretation
* synchronized strategic state remains authoritative

This preserves:

* determinism
* synchronization stability
* bounded architecture

---

# Auditability Rule

Ideological and faction influence should be auditable.

Examples:

* militarist faction pressure increased offensive posture
* pacifist unrest slowed expansion
* spiritualist pressure resisted synthetic ascension
* xenophile support improved federation trust

Strategic evolution should remain:

* explainable
* historically understandable
* ideologically coherent

---

# Relationship To Other Systems

This document acts as a high-level ideological governance layer for:

* personality systems
* doctrine systems
* diplomacy systems
* relationship systems
* memory systems
* ascension systems
* war planning systems

These systems should reference ethics and faction pressure rather than redefining ideological behavior independently.

---

# Design Goal

Strategic Nexus civilizations should feel like:

```text id="d7m4qp"
ideological civilizations balancing strategy, political pressure, memory, ethics, and survival
```

not:

```text id="0x1rks"
perfectly unified optimization machines without internal political identity
```

Ethics and faction pressure are among the core pillars that transform Strategic Nexus from a traditional AI mod into a civilization simulation layer.

---

# Population Ethics Distribution Rule

Official empire ethics must account for actual population ideological distribution.

Civilizations are not ideologically static.

Population ethics may shift because of:

* conquest
* migration
* refugees
* assimilation
* demographic growth
* species integration
* long-term propaganda
* crisis pressure
* technological transformation

Strategic Nexus must treat population ideological composition as strategically important.

---

# Governing Ethics And Stability Rule

Governing ethics strongly affect:

* faction approval
* stability
* happiness
* governing ethics attraction
* internal cohesion
* political pressure
* economic efficiency

This means official ethics are not merely ideological flavor.

Official ethics are one of the core stability and economic systems of the civilization.

---

# Internal Governance Response Rule

Strategic Nexus may influence high-level responses to faction and ethics pressure.

Possible responses:

* suppress faction
* support faction
* tolerate faction
* politically ignore faction
* gradually reform toward faction ethics
* preserve ideological purity
* stabilize multicultural coexistence

The LLM should influence strategic direction, not detailed pop-level micro-management.

---

# Ethics Transition Rule

Civilizations may strategically adapt official ethics if:

* ideological mismatch becomes unsustainable
* faction pressure becomes destabilizing
* population ethics distribution shifts heavily
* conquest radically alters demographics
* migration transforms civilization composition
* long-term stability collapses
* economic efficiency suffers severely

This is a legitimate civilization survival response.

---

# Ethics Shift Inertia Rule

Official ethics shifts must remain:

* rare
* slow
* historically significant
* politically expensive
* strategically disruptive

Ethics shifts should feel like:

* regime transformation
* ideological reform
* civilization evolution

not:

* routine optimization

---

# Ethics Shift Constraint Rule

Strategic Nexus must respect Stellaris mechanics and constraints regarding ethics reform.

Possible constraints include:

* influence cost
* reform cooldowns
* governing reform limitations
* faction support requirements
* political instability risk

The LLM must not assume ethics may be changed freely or frequently.

The scripted/mod layer remains responsible for:

* legality checks
* cooldown validation
* reform execution constraints

---

# Controlled Ethics Transition Principle

The LLM should usually recommend strategic direction rather than direct ethics replacement.

Preferred:

```json id="9p1f5e"
{
  "internal_governance_strategy": "controlled_ethics_transition",
  "ethics_pressure": "materialist_growth_unsustainable",
  "recommended_response": "gradual_reform"
}
```

Avoid:

```json id="m1o4xt"
{
  "change_ethics_now": true
}
```

Strategic Nexus should remain:

* bounded
* gradual
* historically believable

---

# Conquest And Demographic Shock Rule

Large territorial expansion may create ideological instability.

Examples:

* annexation of hostile populations
* refugee influx
* migration treaties
* integration of subject species
* liberation wars
* crisis displacement

These may:

* strengthen factions
* weaken governing ethics
* alter political stability
* force strategic adaptation
* create ethics drift pressure

Civilizations should react differently depending on:

* ethics
* personality
* trauma
* governance strategy
* economic condition

---

# Ethics Versus Efficiency Principle

Sometimes preserving old ideology is too expensive.

A civilization may eventually conclude:

* ideological rigidity threatens survival
* population drift is irreversible
* suppression costs are unsustainable
* economic penalties are too severe

This may justify:

* gradual ethics adaptation
* controlled reform
* ideological softening
* political restructuring

This is desirable long-term civilization behavior.

---

# Personality Interaction Rule

Different personalities should react differently to ideological pressure.

Examples:

Paranoid empire:
→ suppression and ideological rigidity

Pragmatic empire:
→ gradual adaptation

Honor-bound empire:
→ preserve tradition despite instability

Opportunistic empire:
→ rapid political realignment

Technocratic empire:
→ optimize ideological-economic compatibility

---

# Attention And Review Cadence Rule

Faction and ethics governance should update slowly.

Review triggers may include:

* major conquest
* demographic transformation
* refugee crisis
* severe instability
* faction surge
* ethics attraction collapse
* ascension transition

The system must avoid:

* constant ideological reevaluation
* unstable ethics oscillation
* monthly political reform spam

---

# Anti-Optimizer Principle

Strategic Nexus civilizations should not instantly adopt whatever ethics are mathematically optimal.

Civilizations should:

* resist change
* preserve identity
* fear instability
* carry ideological inertia
* react emotionally to reform

This creates:

* believable politics
* historical continuity
* strategic individuality

---

# Design Goal

Strategic Nexus civilizations should feel like:

```text id="n8x3kp"
living political civilizations struggling between ideology, demographics, stability, and survival
```

not:

```text id="1z0fhy"
perfectly adaptive optimization machines instantly changing ideology for efficiency
```
